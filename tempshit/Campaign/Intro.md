In the deep vault of night, where the stars whisper truths older than gods, there hangs a wounded world. Golarion—cracked by prophecy, tempered by catastrophe, and forever haunted by the echo of a single falling stone.
Long ago, when empires still believed themselves eternal, the sky betrayed them. A burning continent of rock tore through the heavens, screaming toward the world in a blaze of impossible light. *Earthfall.* In that moment, the proud Azlanti vanished, the seas rose in fury, and the age of mortals was reset like a shattered hourglass.
Yet from the ruin came a miracle. A shard of that celestial doom—*the Starstone*—did not merely destroy. It transformed. Where it struck, a crater became a crucible, and within it, divinity itself stirred. Heroes who dared its trials emerged as gods, their names etched into the firmament. And among them rose the greatest of all: **Aroden**, the Last Azlanti, the immortal promise that humanity would rise again.
For millennia, his presence steadied the world. His return was prophesied as the dawn of a new golden age. But prophecy is a fragile thing. On the eve of his promised arrival, Aroden simply… vanished. No corpse. No sign. Only silence—so vast and sudden it cracked the very machinery of fate.
The heavens convulsed. Oracles screamed. Storms raged without end. And the Age of Lost Omens began.
Still the Starstone waits in its cathedral of impossible geometry, pulsing like a heart that remembers too much.

The world holds its breath.  
The omens are lost.  
The story is not.

But history is never content to sleep.
In the shadows of Cheliax — beneath its marble temples, its infernal contracts, its ironclad order — another war festers. Not a war of nations, but of faiths. Three powers coil in the dark, each whispering that the world is theirs to claim when the last omen finally fails.
One cult moves with the precision of knives. They believe the death of prophecy is a ledger error waiting to be corrected — and that only absolute order, carved into the bones of the world, can restore it.
Another welcomes the silence left by Aroden’s fall. To them, the unraveling of fate is a hymn, and suffering is the only true scripture.
And deeper still, in caverns where even devils fear to tread, the acolytes of the Rough Beast gnaw at the edges of reality. They do not worship Rovagug for dominion or doctrine. They worship him because they want to watch the world end.
Three cults. Three visions of the future.  
All wrong.
For in the dust of a forgotten monastery, an omen long dismissed as myth has begun to stir again — a prophecy older than Cheliax, as old as the Starstone itself, made by a god which never existed.

It speaks of four souls, born under a sky without omens.  
Four sparks hidden from the eyes of fate.  
Four lives stolen from the cradle and carried into secrecy.  
Four weapons forged in silence for a war no one yet sees.

A prophecy that should have been impossible.  
A prophecy that should have died with Aroden.  
A prophecy that has begun to breathe again.

The world does not know their names.
The gods cannot see their futures.
But the shadows are already moving.

It is past time for the story to wake.
And for them. They've overslept.
